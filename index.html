Who We Are

We are a dedicated organization that transforms the way governments make decisions that affect governance and human rights on which livelihoods depend.

Our Objectives

• Ensuring individual violations of human rights are identified either directly or by supporting particular cases through relevant institutions.
• Providing direct assistance to those whose rights have been violated.
• lobbying for changes to national, regional or international government.
• promoting knowledge of, and respect for, human rights among citizens.

What We Do

Making the highest possible impact on governance and human rights, by finding practical options to shift the way people interact with their governments.

Vision

To establish a community that is humane, just, democratic, transparent, accountable and equitable in which all people can exercise their right to life of dignity.

Mission

To work with poor people and underprivileged to overcome such barriers such as injustice and inequality that causes it.

Goal

To be a key institution in empowering youth and women economically through technical and material support

Values

Solidarity with poor and underprivileged people, fairness, justice and equity, respect for children and women, respect for diversity, honesty, accountability and transparency

Our Story

Pride Economic Empowerment Programme is a registered non-governmental organization having gotten its certification in March 2009. It was founded after the core members who happened to heir from these localities realized of the social woes that inflict the natives and the economic distress that cripple them. These cancerous problems have always dragged the region socially and economically behind despite the national Economic/Development surveys which at times rates the region to be a little bit ahead than other regions. They include but not limited to these; unemployment among the youths, drugs/substances abuse, HIV/Aids prevalence rate being high, poor performances in schools, all amounting to poverty in the region. It’s after a keen observation of these social/economic situations that the members sort for an intervention thus the birth of Pride Economic Empowerment Programme
