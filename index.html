Who We Are

We are a dedicated organization that transforms the way governments make decisions that affect governance and human rights on which livelihoods depend.

Our Objectives

1. Ensuring individual violations of human rights are identified either directly or by supporting particular cases through relevant institutions.
2. Providing direct assistance to those whose rights have been violated.
3. lobbying for changes to national, regional or international government.
4. promoting knowledge of, and respect for, human rights among citizens.

What We Do

Making the highest possible impact on governance and human rights, by finding practical options to shift the way people interact with their governments.

Vision

To establish a community that is humane, just, democratic, transparent, accountable and equitable in which all people can exercise their right to life of dignity.

Mission

To work with poor people and underprivileged to overcome such barriers such as injustice and inequality that causes it.

Goal

To be a key institution in empowering youth and women economically through technical and material support

Values

Solidarity with poor and underprivileged people, fairness, justice and equity, respect for children and women, respect for diversity, honesty, accountability and transparency

OUR PROJECTS

We implement our projects using clear, measurable, and well-structured goals:

Project 1

One of our Community Empowerment Initiative aimed to train local leaders to advocate for their communities. These leaders organized forums where residents could voice their grievances and propose solutions

Project 2

In another we collaborated with schools, providing children with human rights education. Using art and storytelling as tools, they encouraged kids to express their understanding of justice and equality. Schools began to see a cultural shift where students stood up for their peers and challenged bullying and discrimination.

Project 3

We partnered with local and international human rights organizations, this has provided additional resources and expertise, allowing us to amplify our work. We began to participate in global human rights forums, shared our local experiences and gained insights from activists around the world.

Project 4

Currently we are developing a policy advocacy program that target government institutions, urging them to adopt clearer anti-corruption measures to improve public service transparency.

Our Story

PEEP (Pride Economic Empowerment Programme) is a Non-Governmental Organization which was registered in March 2009. It is dedicated to advocating for human rights and improving governance in diverse populations especially marginalized communities which include women and youths. Founded by a group of experts, activists and social workers, the NGO aimed to foster transparency, accountability, and justice through grassroots initiatives and policy reform. The story of PEEP serves as a testament to the power of collective action in confronting human rights abuses and advocating for effective governance. Our journey highlights that the fight for human dignity is ongoing and that with determination, resilience, and collaboration, a brighter and fairer future is attainable for all. We continue to stand as a guardian, not only for the rights of individuals but also for the principles of justice and equity that underpin a thriving society. Despite our successes, we faced numerous obstacles. Corrupt officials and those resistant to change are opposed to our work. There were instances of harassment and intimidation aimed at threatening our mission. However, the resilience of the team shone through. They organized public awareness, rallying citizens to stand in solidarity for their rights. With support from the community, we pushed back against threats, reminding everyone that human rights are not merely privileges but inherent to all people.
